Watching the Sunset in Ronda, Spain

I think I forgot to mention that this crazy day – the one that began in Seville where we managed to pack three big sights into four hours, then took a scenic bus ride to Ronda, followed by an off-track hike through El Tajo Gorge – was also our 11th anniversary. It was a boiling hot day, we were so busy we barely had time to eat, and I ended it with more than a few scrapes and bruises, but if I had the choice, this is how I’d choose to spend my anniversary every year. Especially if those days could end in the same way this one did.

From what I hear, most people who visit Ronda come via day tours from Málaga or other neighboring large cities. Given how small the town is, even if you’ve only got 5-6 hours, you can probably see and do quite a bit in that amount of time, but my problem with day tours is they’re just that – day tours. When you take them, you see nothing of the city/town at evening. That’s why we decided to stay overnight in Ronda, particularly so we could feast our eyes on the sunset in Ronda over the brilliant landscape that I’d heard would knock our socks off, had it been cold enough for us to actually be wearing any.

We had scoped out the area earlier in the day and knew exactly where we wanted to be when our evening entertainment went down (to the right of Puente Nuevo bridge, looking towards Alameda del Tajo), but because of the accidental detour we made on our hike, we almost missed it. The sun was already halfway behind the mountains by the time we reached our look-out point, so I quickly set up a few shots and then spent the remaining time just gazing out at the view, hypnotized by the descent of the sun and the colors it created in the sky.

Just by chance, before the sun’s last rays disappeared beneath the mountains, I glanced at the time. 9:10 PM. I quickly counted back seven hours and then turned to Cory in disbelief. That exact time eleven years ago, we were holding hands and saying our vows underneath the gazebo in my parent’s backyard. Timing that good cannot be planned. I’m pretty sure that sunset was just for us.

We ended our 11th year together in the absolute best possible way. I’m sure our future anniversaries will be celebrated in far less glamorous ways – they can’t all be spent watching the sun go down in a remote town in southern Spain, after all – but I’m hoping we’ll at least have many more chances to try!
